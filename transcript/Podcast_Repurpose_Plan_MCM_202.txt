MCM 202 Transcript Repurposing Plan for a Limited Podcast Lecture Series
Date: 15 April 2026

Goal
Convert transcript_MCM_202 lecture materials into a short, high-impact podcast series that is engaging, academically useful, and easy to record consistently.

Series Concept
Title (working): Editing and Graphics of Communication in Practice | MCM 202 Audio Series
Format: Limited series (8 episodes)
Episode length: 18-28 minutes each
Audience: MCM 202 students (primary), mass communication students and early-career journalists and editors (secondary)
Publishing frequency: 1 episode per week (or 2 per week for accelerated release)
Tone: Conversational lecturer voice + practical storytelling + exam-focused clarity

Why Limited Series (Not Endless)
1. Keeps production realistic and sustainable.
2. Improves completion rate by giving listeners a clear end point.
3. Supports stronger teaching evidence because outcomes can be measured across a defined arc.

Episode Structure Template (Use for Every Episode)
0. Pre-open hook (30-40 sec): MANDATORY. Runs before the opening music. Opens with a single sharp, provocative or high-stakes statement that creates an immediate reason to keep listening. Use an analogy, a professional consequence, a rhetorical question, or a surprising fact rooted in the episode's core concept. No introductions. No titles. Straight into the idea. See Episode 1 for the established model.
1. Cold open (30-60 sec): Start with a real editing error or newsroom case from Nigerian or global media.
2. Lesson promise (20 sec): "By the end of this episode, you will be able to..."
3. Core teaching segments (3 segments, 4-6 min each): Explain + example + quick takeaway.
4. Micro-activity (2 min): "Pause and try this" task from transcript content.
5. Checkpoint question (1 min): 1 exam-style or scenario-based question.
6. Recap (1 min): 3 key points only.
7. Action close (30 sec): Point to next episode, reading, or submission task.

Engagement Design (Make it Lively Without Losing Academic Depth)
1. Use signature recurring segments:
- "Editor's Eye": one practical editing or graphics decision dilemma per episode.
- "Newsroom Snapshot": one real or reconstructed Nigerian newsroom scenario.
- "Symbol Check": one editing symbol or convention listeners must correctly identify or apply.

2. Build active listening moments:
- Ask listeners to pause and correct a headline, caption, or marked copy before you reveal the answer.
- Use "choose A/B" decisions (word choice, image selection, layout priority, or ethical call).

3. Add relevant local context:
- Include examples from Nigerian newspaper and magazine production practices.
- Reference common errors seen in MCM 202 practical submissions.
- Ground discussion in the Nigerian press context where relevant.

4. Keep a clear narrative thread:
- Reuse one "mock news package" case across episodes so students see the full journey from raw copy to designed page.

Script Development Workflow (From Existing Transcript Files)
Step 0: Write the pre-open hook first
- Identify the single most important or surprising idea in the episode.
- Write a 3-5 line hook that opens on that idea without naming the topic or introducing yourself.
- Test it by asking: would someone who has no context still feel compelled to keep listening?
- The hook must end with a forward-pull line that makes the episode feel unmissable.
- Format label in script: [PRE-OPEN HOOK - 35 seconds]

Step 1: Transcript cleanup
- Remove filler words, duplicate phrases, and scanning/QR instructions that do not serve audio-only listeners.
- Correct obvious transcription errors while preserving your teaching voice.

Step 2: Segment extraction
- Pull the strongest teaching blocks from each source transcript.
- Tag each block as: Concept, Example, Activity, or Assessment.

Step 3: Podcast rewrite
- Rewrite into spoken-audio rhythm: shorter sentences, clearer transitions, and stronger openings.
- Add explicit transitions such as "Now let's apply this" and "Here is where students get confused."

Step 4: Episode timing pass
- Read script aloud and trim to target duration.
- Remove overlong definitions and keep one definition + one applied example.

Step 5: Delivery notes
- Mark emphasis words, pause points, and where to sound reflective or energetic.

Recording Plan (Lightweight and Repeatable)
Recording block per episode: 60-90 minutes
- 15 min vocal warm-up + script familiarity
- 25-35 min main recording
- 20-30 min retakes and clean pickups

Technical settings
- Quiet room, fixed mic distance, pop filter if available.
- Record WAV for master archive, export MP3 for distribution.
- Keep intro/outro music short and low under speech.

Post-Production Checklist
1. Remove long silences, loud breaths, and repeated lines.
2. Normalize levels for consistent listening across episodes.
3. Add chapter markers (if platform supports this).
4. Insert short outro call-to-action linked to class activity or QR-based task.

Repurposing Outputs Per Episode (Beyond Audio)
For each published episode, generate:
1. 1 short text summary (150-220 words) for LMS or class group.
2. 3 key terms with definitions.
3. 1 quiz question (MCQ or short answer).
4. 1 practical mini-task (5-10 min).
5. 1 social snippet (30-60 sec quote/clip) for awareness.

Evidence and Metrics Plan (Teaching Documentation)
Track these for each episode:
1. Release date and runtime.
2. Listener count (7-day and 30-day).
3. Average completion rate (if available).
4. Number of student responses to checkpoint question.
5. Most-missed concept in follow-up quiz.

Success indicators after full series:
- At least 70% of enrolled students listen to 5 or more episodes.
- At least 60% average score on episode checkpoint questions.
- Clear reduction in repeated editing and graphics errors in practical submissions.

Week 2: Record and publish Episode 1; draft Episode 2.
Week 3: Publish Episode 2; draft Episode 3.
Week 4: Publish Episode 3 and 4 (or publish Episode 3 only if single-release cadence).
Week 5: Publish Episode 5; run midpoint learner feedback poll.
Week 6: Publish Episode 6.
Week 7: Publish Episode 7.
Week 8: Publish Episode 8; compile engagement metrics and teaching reflection notes.

Risk Management
Risk: Episodes feel too long.
Mitigation: Hard cap at 28 minutes; move extra content to text handout.

Risk: Student passive listening.
Mitigation: Include one mandatory pause activity and one response prompt in every episode.

Risk: Inconsistent production quality.
Mitigation: Use a fixed recording template and post-production checklist.

Quick Start Actions (Do This First)
1. Create one reusable script template based on the Episode Structure Template above.
2. Pilot with Episode 1 using the Lecture 1 + LIVE SESSION transcripts; publish as proof of concept.
3. Collect student feedback after Episode 1 and refine pacing before recording Episodes 2-4.
4. Keep all versions organized by naming convention:
- MCM202_Podcast_E01_Script_v1.txt
- MCM202_Podcast_E01_Record_v1.wav
- MCM202_Podcast_E01_Final.mp3
